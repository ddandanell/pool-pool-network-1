Expert Water Chemistry Management

Maintaining proper water chemistry is the foundation of effective pool maintenance in Bali. Our technicians test and adjust pH levels (ideal range 7.2-7.6), chlorine concentration (1-3 ppm for traditional pools), total alkalinity (80-120 ppm), calcium hardness (200-400 ppm), and cyanuric acid levels (30-50 ppm). These parameters are crucial for water clarity, swimmer comfort, equipment longevity, and preventing common issues like scaling, corrosion, and algae growth.

Seasonal Adaptation for Optimal Pool Health

Bali's climate presents distinct challenges during both wet and dry seasons, and our pool maintenance services adapt accordingly. During the monsoon season from November to March, pools face increased rainfall that dilutes chemicals, introduces contaminants, and can cause overflow. Our wet season maintenance protocol includes more frequent chemical testing (sometimes twice per visit), enhanced debris removal, careful water level management, and increased filtration times to handle the additional load.

The dry season from April to October brings different challenges including accelerated evaporation (pools can lose 3-5cm of water per week), increased dust accumulation, higher UV exposure leading to faster chlorine degradation, and the need for more frequent water additions. Our dry season maintenance adjusts for these factors with increased chlorine stabilizer monitoring, more frequent water top-ups, enhanced surface cleaning to remove dust and pollen, and equipment checks to ensure efficient operation during peak usage periods.

Comprehensive Cleaning Protocols

Physical cleaning is a critical component of our pool maintenance Bali services. Each maintenance visit includes thorough skimming of the surface to remove leaves, flowers, insects, and other debris that constantly fall into pools in Bali's lush environment. We manually brush all pool surfaces including walls, steps, corners, and behind ladders to prevent algae attachment and remove biofilm. Vacuuming removes sediment and debris from the pool floor, whether using manual vacuums for thorough cleaning or servicing automatic pool cleaners.

We pay special attention to the waterline, where oils, sunscreen, and organic matter accumulate to create unsightly rings. Our technicians use specialized tile and grout cleaners that are effective yet gentle on pool surfaces. All skimmer baskets and pump strainer baskets are emptied and cleaned during each visit to ensure optimal water circulation. Filter maintenance, including backwashing sand and DE filters or cleaning cartridge filters, is performed according to manufacturer specifications and pressure gauge readings.

Equipment Inspection and Preventive Care

Pool equipment works harder in Bali's tropical climate, making regular inspection and maintenance crucial for longevity and efficiency. During each pool maintenance visit, our technicians thoroughly inspect your pump and motor, checking for unusual noises, vibrations, leaks, or overheating. We verify proper operation of the filtration system, including pressure gauge readings and filter media condition. All automatic pool cleaners are checked for proper operation, and worn parts are identified for replacement.

We inspect all plumbing connections for leaks or deterioration, particularly important in areas with salt air exposure. Lighting systems are tested for proper operation and safety. Pool heaters and heat pumps receive regular inspection to ensure efficient operation. Chlorine generators (salt cells) in saltwater pools are inspected for calcium buildup and function. Any identified issues are documented and discussed with clients, along with recommendations for repair or replacement before minor problems become major failures.
