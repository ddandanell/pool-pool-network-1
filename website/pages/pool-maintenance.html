Expert Water Chemistry Management

Maintaining proper water chemistry is the foundation of effective pool maintenance in Bali. Our technicians test and adjust pH levels (ideal range 7.2-7.6), chlorine concentration (1-3 ppm for traditional pools), total alkalinity (80-120 ppm), calcium hardness (200-400 ppm), and cyanuric acid levels (30-50 ppm). These parameters are crucial for water clarity, swimmer comfort, equipment longevity, and preventing common issues like scaling, corrosion, and algae growth.

Seasonal Adaptation for Optimal Pool Health

Bali's climate presents distinct challenges during both wet and dry seasons, and our pool maintenance services adapt accordingly. During the monsoon season from November to March, pools face increased rainfall that dilutes chemicals, introduces contaminants, and can cause overflow. Our wet season maintenance protocol includes more frequent chemical testing (sometimes twice per visit), enhanced debris removal, careful water level management, and increased filtration times to handle the additional load.

The dry season from April to October brings different challenges including accelerated evaporation (pools can lose 3-5cm of water per week), increased dust accumulation, higher UV exposure leading to faster chlorine degradation, and the need for more frequent water additions. Our dry season maintenance adjusts for these factors with increased chlorine stabilizer monitoring, more frequent water top-ups, enhanced surface cleaning to remove dust and pollen, and equipment checks to ensure efficient operation during peak usage periods.

Equipment Inspection and Preventive Care

Pool equipment works harder in Bali's tropical climate, making regular inspection and maintenance crucial for longevity and efficiency. During each pool maintenance visit, our technicians thoroughly inspect your pump and motor, checking for unusual noises, vibrations, leaks, or overheating.
